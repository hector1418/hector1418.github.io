History of the different clubs

Athletic Club

1890-1899 Athletic Club is born British football arrives in Bilbao via the Ría. The love of a new sport Soccer arose in Bizkaia during the industrialization experienced by Bilbao and its surroundings at the end of the 19th century. Britons living in the territory and young people from Biscay who have studied on the Islands are its precursors. Enthusiasm for the new sport prompted the birth of the Athletic Club in 1898.

Athletico Madrid

Club Atlético de Madrid is a Spanish football club, from the city of Madrid. It was founded on April 26, 1903 and currently plays in the First Division of Spain. Atlético de Madrid is the third club with the most national league titles (with 11 titles), behind Real Madrid and FC Barcelona.

Elche

Elche Club de Fútbol is a Spanish football club from the city of Elche, Valencian Community, which competes in the First Division of Spain. It was founded on August 28, 1922, from the merger of several Elche clubs. Its first team plays its home matches at the Estadio Martínez Valero, with capacity for 31,388 spectators.

barcelona

The Fútbol Club Barcelona (in Catalan, Futbol Club Barcelona), popularly known as Barça, is a sports entity based in Barcelona, Spain. It was founded as a football club on November 29, 1899 and officially registered on January 5, 1903.

Getafe

Getafe Club de Fútbol, ​​S. A. D. is a Spanish football club based in the city of Getafe, in the Province of Madrid. Play in the First Division of Spain. It was the 58th team to join the First Division, its first participation being in the 2004-05 season. Since the 2021-22 season, it has been ranked 22nd in the historical classification of the highest category.

Girona

Soccer arrived in Girona at the beginning of the 20th century. The first great club in the city was the historic Strong Esport (1902 with the original name of FC Gerundense). Other clubs from these early years were the Agrupación Juventud (1903), Girona Sport (the seminarians), Atlético (of the working class), the Club Patronato (social-Catholic), and other less prominent clubs such as Gerunda, Olímpico , Catalònia, Student and Industrial. The first football matches in the city were played in the field of Marte de la Devesa.

Rayo Vallecano

El Rayo was founded on May 29, 1924, at the home of Doña Prudencia Priego, Huerta's widow. First president: Julián Huerta. The team's original uniform was a white shirt and shorts and black socks with white cuffs. In the 1949-50 season, a collaboration agreement was signed with Atlético de Madrid, which caused the uniform to be changed and a red diagonal stripe added to the shirt. These colors have been maintained to this day.

Celta

Celta de Vigo was founded by Manuel de Castro "Handicap" and Juan Baliño on August 23, 1923, by merging the 2 clubs from the city of Vigo (Pontevedra): Vigo Sporting and Real Fortuna. Vigo Sporting was the most important in the city, as it was runner-up in the Copa del Rey in 1908. The first president of Celta de Vigo was Manuel Bárcena y de Andrés, Count of Torre Cedeira.

Espanyol

The Real Club Deportivo Espanyol de Barcelona (in Catalan and officially, Reial Club Deportiu Espanyol de Barcelona) is a football club based in Barcelona, Spain. Although its origins date from the year 1900 under the name of the Spanish Football Society—, it was officially established as a football club in Barcelona on February 28, 1909 after a restructuring of various sports clubs under the name of Club Deportivo Español.

Mallorca

Manolo Villanova was going to be in charge of fighting for the return to First Division. Goalkeeper for Mallorca in the 66-67 and 67-68 seasons, he had a proven squad in which, among others, Manolo Preciado, Pep Bonet, Damià Amer, Antonio Orejuela, Paco Higuera and Gerry Armstrong stood out. However, it was not enough to get promoted and Mallorca would have to wait another year.

Betis

In 1907, a group of students from the Polytechnic School, located on Cervantes street, who were studying preparatory studies for a military career and for medicine, founded Sevilla Balompié. Among them were many groups of brothers: the Hermosas, Wesolowskis, Castillos, Cascales, and Gutierrez. They dressed in white and blue. Its first president was Alfonso del Castillo Ochoa and the first captain -who served as coach-, in 1914, Manuel Ramos Asencio.

Real Madrid

Real Madrid Club de Fútbol, better known simply as Real Madrid, is a sports entity based in Madrid, Spain. It was declared officially registered as a football club by its members on March 6, 1902 for the purpose of practicing and developing this sport, although its origins date from the year 1900, and its name (Society) Madrid Football Club de October 1901, being the fifth club founded in the capital.

Real Sociedad

Real Sociedad de Fútbol, better known as Real Sociedad or simply La Real, is a Spanish football club based in San Sebastián, Guipúzcoa. It was founded on September 7, 1909, although its origins date back to 1904 with the former San Sebastián Recreation Club society, of which several members went on to form the San Sebastián Foot-Ball Club in 1907.

Valladolid

On June 20, 1928, Real Valladolid Deportivo was born from the merger of two Valladolid clubs: Real Unión Deportiva and Club Deportivo Español. Their first match was held on September 22, 1928 at the Campo de la Sociedad Taurina (next to the Plaza de Toros) against Alavés, with a 2-1 victory. The first goal of that match and in the history of Real Valladolid Deportivo was scored by Sainz. The first president was Pedro Zuloaga Mañueco and the first coach, the Hungarian Esteban Platko.

Sevilla

On January 25, 1890, a group of young people, half British and half Spanish, founded the Sevilla Football Club. Edward Johnston, Isaías White and Hugo MacColl will be president, secretary and captain respectively. The Seville Football Club was duly constituted and with its official elected positions These words appeared on March 17, 1890 in The Dundee Courier (Scotland). The article shows the date of January 25, 1890 as the constitution of the club, as in accordance with current legislation (Civil Code).

Almería

The Unión Deportiva Almería is a football club from the city of Almería, in the province of Almería, Spain. It was founded on July 26, 1989 under the name of Almería Club de Fútbol, adopting the current name in 2001, which meant only a change of initials, since the club continued to be the same by maintaining its original federative number since 1989. Currently plays in the First Division of Spain.

Valencia

Valencia Club de Fútbol S. A. D. is a Spanish football club from the city of Valencia (Valencian Community, Spain). It was founded on March 18, 1919 and plays in the First Division of Spain. It disputes the matches at home in the Mestalla Stadium with a capacity for 48,600 spectators. In the historical classification of LaLiga, Valencia CF occupies 4th place1 behind Real Madrid, FC Barcelona and Atlético de Madrid. It is located in the 25th place of the richest clubs in the world, with 99.3 million euros, being the 5th Spanish club on the list.

Villarreal

Villarreal Club de Fútbol, S. A D. is a Spanish football club from the city of Villarreal, in the province of Castellón, which plays in the First Division. It disputes its matches at the Estadio de la Cerámica, with a capacity for 22,000 spectators. Since the 1940s, the club's clothing has been characterized by the yellow color of its shirt, and the team is popularly known under the nickname "Submarino Amarillo" or "Submarí Groguet" (in Valencian).
